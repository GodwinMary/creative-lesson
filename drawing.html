Types of Lines

Lines are the foundation of all drawings. Learn the different types of lines used in art:

Horizontal Line

Runs left to right. Suggests calmness and stability.

Vertical Line

Runs up and down. Suggests strength and height.

Diagonal Line

Runs at an angle. Suggests movement and energy.

Curved Line

Flows smoothly. Suggests softness and grace.

Zigzag Line

Sharp angles create excitement and energy.

Perspective Drawing

Perspective makes drawings look realistic by showing depth. Objects far away appear smaller.

  • 1. Draw a horizon line across your page
  • 2. Mark the vanishing point (VP)
  • 3. Draw lines from VP to bottom corners
  • 4. Add objects that get smaller near the VP

Shading Techniques

Gradient Shading

Smooth transition from dark to light

Hatching

Parallel lines, closer = darker

Cross-Hatching

Overlapping lines for deeper shade

Stippling

Using dots, more dots = darker

Proportion Guide

Proportion is about the correct size relationship between parts of a drawing.

  • • Eyes are halfway down the head
  • • Nose is halfway between eyes and chin
  • • A person is about 8 heads tall
  • • Arms reach to mid-thigh

Pro Tip: Use light guidelines to plan proportions before adding detail. You can erase them later!

Warm vs Cool Colors

Warm Colors

Suggest heat, energy, and excitement. They appear to come forward in a painting.

Cool Colors

Suggest calm, peace, and nature. They appear to recede in a painting.

Colors in Nigerian Art

Colors hold deep meaning in Nigerian culture and traditional art:

Red

Vitality, power, danger. Used in masquerade costumes and royal regalia.

White

Purity, peace, spirituality. Common in Igbo ceremonial chalk (nzu).

Green

Life, fertility, prosperity. Represents agriculture and the lush landscape.

Yellow / Gold

Wealth, royalty, harvest. Found in Benin bronzes and royal Yoruba crowns.

Indigo Blue

Love, harmony, unity. The signature color of Yoruba Adire textile dyeing.

Black

Maturity, ancestors, the spiritual realm. Used in Nsibidi symbols.

Practice Activities

Challenge yourself with these drawing prompts inspired by Nigerian culture!

JSS 1-3 Creative

Draw a Traditional Nigerian Mask

Study the symmetry and patterns of traditional masks from the Igbo, Yoruba, or Edo people. Use bold lines and geometric shapes.

JSS 2-3 Pattern

Create an Adire Pattern

Design your own Adire-inspired pattern using lines, circles, and geometric shapes. Use blue and white colors for authenticity.

JSS 1 Observation

Draw Your School

Use basic shapes and perspective to draw your school building. Include details like windows, doors, and the school sign.

JSS 2 Cultural

Design a Decorated Calabash

Draw and decorate a calabash (gourd) with traditional Hausa or Fulani geometric patterns and motifs.
