What is Drama?

Drama is the art of telling stories through live performance. Actors use speech, movement, gestures, and emotions to bring characters and stories to life on stage.

Key Elements of Drama

  • 1.
    Plot — The story or sequence of events in a play
  • 2.
    Characters — The people (actors) who carry out the action
  • 3.
    Dialogue — The words spoken by characters
  • 4.
    Setting — Where and when the story takes place
  • 5.
    Theme — The central message or lesson of the play
  • 6.
    Conflict — The struggle or problem that drives the story

Nigerian Heritage

Nigerian Theatre Pioneers

Meet the legends who shaped Nigerian drama and theatre

🎭

Hubert Ogunde

Father of Nigerian Theatre

Pioneer of Yoruba professional theatre. He founded the first professional theatrical company in Nigeria in 1945 and used drama to address social and political issues.

Famous Works

Tiger's Empire, Yoruba Ronu, Bread and Bullet

🎪

Duro Ladipo

Master of Yoruba Opera

Created folk operas based on Yoruba mythology and history. His works blended traditional music, dance, and drama in spectacular theatrical productions.

Famous Works

Oba Koso, Oba Moro, Oba Waja (Yoruba Trilogy)

🏆

Wole Soyinka

Nobel Laureate in Literature

Africa's first Nobel Prize winner in Literature (1986). A playwright, poet, and essayist whose works explore the human condition through a uniquely African lens.

Famous Works

Death and the King's Horseman, The Lion and the Jewel, A Dance of the Forests

🎬

Ola Rotimi

Master Dramatist & Director

A brilliant playwright known for adapting Greek classical tragedies into African contexts. He was also an exceptional director and theatre administrator at the University of Ife.

Famous Works

The Gods Are Not to Blame, Our Husband Has Gone Mad Again, Kurunmi

Drama Skills by JSS Level

Build your drama skills progressively through each level

🤸

Mime & Movement

Learn to tell stories using only your body — no words needed!

  • Body language & posture
  • Facial expressions (happy, sad, angry, surprised)
  • Hand gestures & sign language basics
  • Movement exercises (walking, running, climbing)
  • Mirror games & follow-the-leader
🎭

Role Play Basics

Step into someone else's shoes and experience different perspectives.

  • What is role play?
  • Playing everyday characters (teacher, doctor, farmer)
  • Voice projection & diction
  • Simple dialogues & scenes
  • Working with a partner
💡

Improvisation

Create scenes on the spot — think fast and be creative!

  • What is improvisation (improv)?
  • "Yes, and..." technique
  • Quick-thinking exercises
  • Group storytelling circles
  • Freeze frame activities
🎪

Stage Craft

Discover how to create the magic behind the scenes.

  • Set design & stage decoration
  • Costumes & wardrobe
  • Props & their uses
  • Stage makeup basics
  • Lighting & sound effects
📝

Scriptwriting Basics

Learn how to write your own scripts and bring stories to the stage.

  • Script format & structure
  • Writing dialogue that sounds natural
  • Stage directions
  • Creating scenes & acts
  • Reading scripts aloud
🧑‍🎤

Character Development

Build believable, memorable characters for the stage.

  • Understanding character motivation
  • Creating character backstories
  • Voice, accent & mannerisms
  • Emotional range & expression
  • Staying in character
🎬

Full Production

Put it all together and stage a complete theatrical production!

  • Directing a play
  • Stage management & coordination
  • The rehearsal process
  • Audience engagement techniques
  • Handling stage fright

Nigerian Folktales for Drama

These beloved Nigerian folktales make wonderful plays. Read the story, then act it out with your classmates!

The Tortoise and the Birds

Tortoise tricks the birds into lending him feathers so he can fly to a feast in the sky. But his greed leads to his downfall when the birds take back their feathers and he falls to earth, cracking his shell.

Characters Needed

Tortoise, 4-6 Birds, Narrator, Sky People

Moral

Greed and dishonesty always lead to trouble

Why the Bat Flies at Night

Bat keeps switching sides between the birds and the animals during their great war, claiming to belong to whichever side is winning. When peace comes, neither side trusts him, so he hides in shame and only comes out at night.

Characters Needed

Bat, Eagle (bird leader), Lion (animal leader), Narrator, Birds group, Animals group

Moral

Disloyalty and indecision earn nobody's trust

The Greedy Monkey

Monkey finds a magic pot that produces food. Instead of sharing with the village, he keeps it all for himself. The pot eventually stops working because of his selfishness, and he learns the value of generosity.

Characters Needed

Monkey, Village Chief, 3-4 Villagers, Narrator, Old Wise Woman

Moral

Sharing brings abundance; greed brings loss
