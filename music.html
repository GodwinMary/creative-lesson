Nigerian Musical Instruments

Discover the traditional instruments that define Nigerian music

🥁

Talking Drum (Dundun)

Yoruba

An hourglass-shaped drum that can mimic the tone and rhythm of Yoruba speech. It is one of the oldest instruments in West Africa.

How it's played: Held under the arm, the player squeezes the strings connecting the two drumheads to change pitch while striking with a curved stick.
🎶

Shekere

Yoruba

A gourd covered with a net of beads or cowrie shells. It produces a shaking, rattling sound that is essential in Yoruba ceremonial music.

How it's played: Shaken, tossed, or struck against the hands. The beaded net slides over the gourd to create rhythmic patterns.
🪵

Ekwe

Igbo

A wooden slit drum carved from a single log. It was traditionally used for communication across Igbo villages and during festivals.

How it's played: Struck with wooden sticks on the slit opening. Different positions on the slit produce different tones and messages.
🔔

Ogene

Igbo

A large metal gong made from iron. It is a key instrument in Igbo traditional music, used in ceremonies, masquerade festivals, and celebrations.

How it's played: Struck with a wooden stick. The open end can be muted with the hand to create different sound effects.
🎺

Kakaki

Hausa

A long metal trumpet, sometimes up to 2 metres long. It is a royal instrument used in Hausa emirate courts to announce the presence of an Emir.

How it's played: Blown from one end like a trumpet. It produces a deep, resonant sound that carries over long distances.
🪘

Gangan

Yoruba

A smaller version of the talking drum family. The Gangan is the lead drum in many Yoruba ensembles and can replicate the tones of Yoruba language.

How it's played: Tucked under the arm and squeezed to change pitch. Played with a curved stick called "opa" to create melodic rhythms.
🏺

Udu Drum

Igbo

A clay pot with an additional hole on the side. Originally a water jug, Igbo women discovered its musical potential and it became a ceremonial instrument.

How it's played: The player strikes the side hole, the top opening, and the body with fingers and palms to produce deep, resonant bass tones.

Music Theory Lessons

Learn music theory at your level

🎵

Musical Notes

In music, there are 7 main notes that form the basis of all melodies. These notes are named after letters of the alphabet:

C D E F G A B

These repeat in higher and lower octaves. Between some notes, there are sharps (#) and flats (b) - giving us 12 notes total in Western music.

📝

The Musical Staff

The staff (or stave) is a set of 5 horizontal lines where musical notes are written. Notes are placed on the lines or in the spaces between them.

𝄞

Treble Clef on a Staff

Lines (bottom to top): E, G, B, D, F - Remember: "Every Good Boy Deserves Fudge"

Spaces (bottom to top): F, A, C, E - They spell "FACE"

⏱️

Time Signatures

Time signatures tell us how many beats are in each bar (measure) of music and what type of note gets one beat.

4/4

Common Time

4 beats per bar

3/4

Waltz Time

3 beats per bar

2/4

March Time

2 beats per bar

6/8

Compound Time

6 beats per bar

Nigerian Traditional Music Forms

Nigeria has a rich tradition of music that reflects its diverse cultures. Traditional music serves many purposes:

Work Songs

Sung during farming, fishing, and other communal activities to keep a steady rhythm and boost morale.

Ceremonial Music

Performed at weddings, festivals, chieftaincy installations, and religious events.

Lullabies

Gentle songs sung to children, often carrying moral lessons and cultural values.

Praise Songs

Songs honouring important people, ancestors, or deities. Common in Yoruba and Hausa traditions.

🎼

Musical Scales

A scale is a sequence of notes arranged in ascending or descending order. Scales form the foundation of melodies.

Major Scale (Happy sound)

C - D - E - F - G - A - B

Pattern: Whole-Whole-Half-Whole-Whole-Whole-Half

Minor Scale (Sad sound)

A - B - C - D - E - F - G

Pattern: Whole-Half-Whole-Whole-Half-Whole-Whole

Pentatonic Scale (5 notes, common in African music)

C - D - E - G - A

Used widely in traditional African and Nigerian music

Types of Music

🎻

Classical Music

Formal Western art music. Composers include Beethoven and Mozart. Uses orchestra instruments.

🏡

Folk Music

Traditional music passed down through generations. Each Nigerian ethnic group has unique folk songs.

🎤

Pop Music

Popular contemporary music. Nigerian pop (Afrobeats) is now a global phenomenon.

🎷

Highlife

Originated in Ghana and Nigeria. Blends traditional rhythms with Western instruments. Pioneers: Bobby Benson, Rex Lawson.

🥁

Juju Music

Yoruba popular music using talking drums, guitars, and vocals. King Sunny Ade is the legendary king of Juju.

✊

Afrobeat

Created by Fela Kuti. Combines jazz, funk, and Yoruba rhythms with political lyrics.

✍️

Composition Basics

Music composition is the art of creating original music. Here are the key elements every composer needs to understand:

Melody

The main tune - a sequence of single notes that form the recognisable part of a song.

Harmony

Multiple notes played together (chords) that support the melody and add depth.

Rhythm

The pattern of beats and timing that drives the music forward.

Dynamics

Variations in loudness - from piano (soft) to forte (loud).

Tempo

The speed of the music - from largo (very slow) to presto (very fast).

🏗️

Song Structure

Most songs follow a structure that organises the music into sections:

INTRO

The opening section that sets the mood and introduces the musical theme.

VERSE

Tells the story. The melody stays the same, but the lyrics change each time to advance the narrative.

CHORUS

The main hook - the most memorable, catchy part of the song. Same words and melody each time it repeats.

BRIDGE

A contrasting section that provides variety and builds tension before returning to the chorus.

OUTRO

The closing section that brings the song to an end, often by fading out or resolving the melody.

Common Pattern: Intro - Verse - Chorus - Verse - Chorus - Bridge - Chorus - Outro

Famous Nigerian & African Musicians

Fela Anikulapo Kuti

Father of Afrobeat

Created the Afrobeat genre by fusing jazz, funk, and Yoruba rhythms. He was also a political activist who used music to fight for the rights of everyday Nigerians. His band "Africa 70" became legendary worldwide.

King Sunny Ade

King of Juju Music

Popularised Juju music internationally with his innovative use of the talking drum combined with modern instruments. He was one of the first African musicians nominated for a Grammy Award.

Miriam Makeba

Mama Africa (South Africa)

A South African singer known as "Mama Africa" who used her music to fight against apartheid. Her song "Pata Pata" became a global hit. She championed African music on the world stage and inspired generations of Nigerian artists.

Music Genres of Nigeria

Nigeria is one of the world's most musically diverse countries

🎷

Highlife

Originated in the early 20th century. Blends traditional Akan and Yoruba rhythms with Western instruments like brass and guitar.

Key Artists: Bobby Benson, Rex Lawson, Victor Olaiya

🥁

Juju Music

Yoruba popular music featuring talking drums, electric guitars, and melodic vocals. Evolved from palm wine music.

Key Artists: King Sunny Ade, Ebenezer Obey

🎺

Afrobeat

Created by Fela Kuti in the 1970s. Fuses jazz, funk, and traditional Yoruba music with politically charged lyrics.

Key Artists: Fela Kuti, Tony Allen, Femi Kuti

🪘

Apala

Traditional Yoruba percussion music originally performed to wake up worshippers during Ramadan. Uses agidigbo and talking drums.

Key Artists: Haruna Ishola, Ayinla Omowura

🎤

Fuji

Evolved from Apala and Were music. A fast-paced Yoruba genre with intense percussion, vocals, and synthesizers added in modern times.

Key Artists: Barrister, Wasiu Ayinde (K1 De Ultimate)

🙏

Gospel

Christian religious music that blends Western gospel traditions with Nigerian languages and rhythms. One of the most popular genres in Nigeria.

Key Artists: Sinach, Nathaniel Bassey, Frank Edwards

🎧

Hip-Hop & Afrobeats

Modern Nigerian hip-hop and Afrobeats (different from Fela's Afrobeat) have taken the world by storm. Blending rap, R&B, dancehall, and Nigerian rhythms, this genre has produced global superstars.

Key Artists: Wizkid, Burna Boy, Davido, Tiwa Savage, Rema
